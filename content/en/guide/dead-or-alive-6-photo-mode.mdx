<Section icon={Camera} title="Advanced Photography Techniques">

To truly excel in the **dead or alive 6 photo mode**, you need to look beyond simple snapshots. The game offers several "Professional" settings that mimic real-world DSLR photography. These settings allow you to manipulate light, focus, and mood to create cinematic images.

One of the most powerful tools at your disposal is the Depth of Field (DoF) setting. By adjusting the aperture, you can blur the background, making the character "pop" from the environment. This is particularly effective in busy stages like "The Throwdown" or "Forbidden Fortune."

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pause the Action">
    Find the exact frame where a strike connects or a character performs a dramatic pose. Use the frame-by-frame advance feature for precision.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust the Aperture">
    Lower the F-stop value to create a shallow depth of field. This blurs the background and focuses the viewer's eye on the fighter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Apply Color Filters">
    Choose from presets like Sepia, Monochrome, or Vivid. Each filter changes the emotional weight of the scene.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Set the Gaze">
    Use the "Look at Camera" toggle. This forces the character's eyes to track the lens, creating a more engaging and personal portrait.
  </Step>
</Steps>
