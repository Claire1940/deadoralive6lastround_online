<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide: Mastering the Triangle System">

If the **dead or alive 6 release date** is your first foray into the series, understanding the "Triangle System" is more important than memorizing long combos. DOA6 is a game of "reads" where every move has a direct counter.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Learn the Triangle Rules">
    Memorize the hierarchy: **Strikes beat Throws**, **Throws beat Holds**, and **Holds beat Strikes**. If you predict your opponent will try to counter your punch with a Hold, use a Throw to deal massive "Hi-Counter" damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize the Special Button (S)">
    The 'S' button is your gateway to new mechanics. Pressing S while moving sideways performs a **Side Attack**, which evades linear strikes. Tapping S four times executes a **Fatal Rush**, a simple but effective combo for beginners.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage your Break Gauge">
    The blue bar under your health is the Break Gauge. At 50%, you can perform a **Break Hold** (Back + S) to escape any strike regardless of height. At 100%, you can unleash a **Break Blow** (Forward + S), a powerful cinematic strike that can parry incoming attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Master High/Mid/Low Defense">
    Unlike other fighters, you must hold the correct height. **High Holds** stop high kicks/punches, **Mid Holds** are split between Punches and Kicks, and **Low Holds** stop sweeps. Practice identifying these animations in Command Training.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Training Path">
The best progression for new players is: **Tutorial** > **DOA Quest** > **Command Training**. DOA Quest is particularly useful as it rewards you with Owner Points and costumes for completing specific combat challenges.
</Callout>
