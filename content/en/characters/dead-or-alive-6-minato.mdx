<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Mechanics: Mastering the Triangle System">

The core of **Dead or Alive 6 Minato** gameplay revolves around the "Triangle System." This rock-paper-scissors mechanic ensures that every offensive action has a specific defensive counter. Understanding this hierarchy is the first step toward moving beyond basic button mashing.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Strikes beat Throws">
    If an opponent attempts to grab you, any basic punch or kick will interrupt the throw and deal counter-hit damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Throws beat Holds">
    If an opponent is waiting to counter your strike with a Hold (Counter), a well-timed Throw will deal massive "Hi-Counter" damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Holds beat Strikes">
    When an opponent is being aggressive with predictable strike strings, using the correct High, Mid, or Low Hold will neutralize their attack.
  </Step>
</Steps>

In addition to the Triangle System, the **Break Gauge** is a critical resource introduced in this generation. It allows for two powerful maneuvers: the **Break Blow** (a high-damage strike that parries incoming attacks) and the **Break Hold** (a universal counter that works against any strike height).

| Mechanic | Gauge Cost | Input (Default) | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fatal Rush** | 0% | S, S, S, S | Simple 4-hit combo ending in stun |
| **Break Blow** | 100% | Forward + S | High-damage strike with armor |
| **Break Hold** | 50% | Back + S | Universal counter for all strike heights |
| **Side Attack** | 0% | Up/Down + S | Sidestep strike to evade linear moves |

<Callout type="warning" title="Gauge Management">
Do not waste your Break Gauge on a Break Blow if the opponent is at low health. Saving 50% for a **Break Hold** is often more valuable as it provides a guaranteed escape from a lethal combo.
</Callout>
