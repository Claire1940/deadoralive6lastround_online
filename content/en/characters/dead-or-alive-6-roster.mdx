<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide & Core Control Layouts">

Dead or Alive 6 is built around five primary actions: **Punch (P)**, **Kick (K)**, **Hold (H)**, **Throw (T)**, and **Special (S)** [homepage-modules-1]. Rather than memorizing massive command lists right away, beginners should focus on understanding the core control layouts and the fundamental "Triangle System" that governs every interaction in the arena [homepage-modules-1].

The **Triangle System** is a rock-paper-scissors mechanic that determines damage scaling:
1. **Strikes beat Throws**: If an opponent tries to throw you while you are executing a punch or kick, your strike will land and deal extra counter damage [homepage-modules-1].
2. **Throws beat Holds**: If an opponent tries to fish for a defensive hold, executing a throw will bypass their guard and deal massive counter-throw damage [homepage-modules-1].
3. **Holds beat Strikes**: If an opponent is mash-striking predictably, executing a well-timed hold matching the strike's height will intercept the attack and counter them [homepage-modules-1].

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Memorize the Default Buttons">
    Familiarize yourself with the primary layout on your chosen platform before diving into character-specific combos [homepage-modules-1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Triangle System">
    Learn to read your opponent's habits. If they block excessively, throw them. If they try to hold, use heavy throws [homepage-modules-1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Practice Multi-Height Defense">
    Standing guard blocks high and mid strikes, while crouching guard blocks low strikes [homepage-modules-1]. Match your holds to the attack height (High, Mid Punch, Mid Kick, Low) [homepage-modules-1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Incorporate the Special Button">
    Use the Special button to execute sidesteps, Side Attacks to evade linear strikes, and simple Fatal Rush strings [homepage-modules-1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Optimize Break Gauge Spending">
    Save 50% gauge for an emergency Break Hold to escape stuns, or use 100% gauge for a high-damage, armor-piercing Break Blow [homepage-modules-1].
  </Step>
</Steps>
