<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat System and Triangle Mechanics">

At the heart of the series lies the **Triangle System**, a rock-paper-scissors mechanic that governs every offensive and defensive interaction [0]. Unlike traditional 2D fighters that rely heavily on blocking and frame traps, this game emphasizes active defensive reads [0]. Successfully executing a counter-read awards a **Counter Hit** or **Hi-Counter Hit**, significantly boosting your damage output [0].

Understanding how these mechanics interact is essential for surviving online lobby matches [0].

| Action | Beats | Loses To | Combat Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strikes (P/K)** | Throws | Holds | Initiates offensive pressure and critical stuns |
| **Throws (T)** | Holds | Strikes | Punishes defensive turtling and active hold attempts |
| **Holds (H)** | Strikes | Throws | Interrupts incoming strings and reverses momentum |

To transition from button-mashing to deliberate, tactical play, follow this structured practice progression [0]:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Memorize the Default Button Layout">
    Familiarize yourself with the five core actions: Punch, Kick, Hold, Throw, and Special [0]. Practice these on your controller or keyboard until muscle memory takes over [0].
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master Guard Heights">
    Learn to alternate between standing guard (which blocks high and mid strikes) and crouching guard (which blocks low strikes) [0]. Remember that guarding leaves you vulnerable to throws [0].
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Learn the Four-Way Hold System">
    Holds must match the incoming strike height and type [0]. Practice executing High Holds, Mid Punch Holds, Mid Kick Holds, and Low Holds in reaction to common opponent strings [0].
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Your Break Gauge">
    Your Break Gauge fills as you deal and receive damage [0]. At 50%, you can execute a universal **Break Hold** to escape any strike [0]. At 100%, perform a **Break Blow** to strike through an opponent's attack [0].
  </Step>
</Steps>
