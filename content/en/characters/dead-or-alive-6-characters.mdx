<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering the Combat: The Triangle System">

To succeed with any of the **dead or alive 6 characters**, you must master the core mechanics that define the series. The combat is built on a "rock-paper-scissors" foundation known as the Triangle System.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Learn the Triangle Rules">
    Strikes beat Throws, Throws beat Holds, and Holds beat Strikes. Winning an interaction in this triangle results in a "Hi Counter," dealing significantly more damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manage the Break Gauge">
    The meter at the top fills as you fight. Use 50% for a **Break Hold** (a universal counter) or 100% for a **Break Blow** (a powerful, cinematic strike that parries incoming hits).
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize Fatal Rush">
    By pressing the Special button (R1/RB) repeatedly, your character performs a simple combo. The final hit can transition into a Break Blow if your meter is full.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Exploit Danger Zones">
    Positioning is key. Knocking an opponent into explosive floors, walls, or stage hazards like the "Rumble Danger" crowd can extend your combos and maximize damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Action | Input (PS/Xbox) | Best Used When... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Break Hold** | Back + Special | You are trapped in a stun or combo. |
| **Break Blow** | Forward + Special | The opponent is mid-attack and you have 100% meter. |
| **Side Attack** | Up/Down + Special | Avoiding linear, straight-line strikes. |
| **Fatal Rush** | Special (Repeated) | You need a reliable, simple combo confirm. |

<Callout type="success" title="Beginner Advice">
Don't mash the Special button. While Fatal Rush is easy to use, it is highly predictable. Mix in standard punches and kicks to keep your opponent guessing.
</Callout>

</Section>
