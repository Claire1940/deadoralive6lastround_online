Rachel’s fighting style, **Counter Fiend Combat**, is characterized by its heavy-hitting nature. Unlike speed-based characters like Kasumi or Ayane, Rachel relies on her ability to absorb pressure and respond with overwhelming force. Her air throws are particularly notorious, as they can catch jumping opponents or be used as finishers for high-launch combos.

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Rachel's Move List">

To truly master **Dead or Alive 6 Rachel**, you must understand the synergy between her strikes and her grappling options. She is not a traditional grappler like Bass; she is a "hybrid" who uses strikes to set up her most powerful throws.

**Key Move Categories:**
1.  **Launchers**: Used to get the opponent into the air for follow-up air throws.
2.  **Stunners**: Strikes that leave the opponent in a "crumple" or "stagger" state, making them vulnerable to offensive holds.
3.  **Ground Throws**: Specialized moves used when the opponent is downed to prevent them from recovering safely.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate the Stun">
    Use mid-level kicks or heavy punches to induce a Critical Stun on the opponent. This prevents them from blocking the next move.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Execute the Launcher">
    Once stunned, use your primary launcher (often a 7K or similar input) to send the opponent skyward.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Air Throw Finisher">
    As the opponent reaches the apex of their flight, input the air throw command to slam them back into the ground for massive damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Map} title="Advanced Tactics and Matchups">

In competitive play, Rachel must be played with patience. Against "rushdown" characters like Mila or Jann Lee, your goal is to use your **Counter Fiend Combat** to interrupt their flow. Use your superior reach to keep them at bay, and use your weight to resist being launched easily.

Against other heavyweights, the battle becomes one of "footsies" and frame data knowledge. You need to know which of their moves leave them at a disadvantage so you can capitalize with a throw. Rachel’s air throw capability gives her a slight edge over other grapplers who lack strong anti-air options.
