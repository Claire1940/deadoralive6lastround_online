<Section icon={Target} title="The Triangle System and Combat Mechanics">

The foundation of **Dead or Alive 6 Core Fighters** is the Triangle System. This rock-paper-scissors mechanic ensures that every offensive action has a dedicated defensive counter, preventing "button mashing" from becoming a viable strategy at higher levels of play. Success in DOA6 requires reading your opponent's tendencies and reacting with the appropriate counter-measure.

| Action | Beats | Loses To | Result of Success |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strikes (P/K)** | Throws | Holds | Deals damage/initiates stun |
| **Throws (T)** | Holds | Strikes | High damage "Hi Counter" |
| **Holds (H)** | Strikes | Throws | Defensive counter-attack |

Beyond the Triangle System, players must manage the **Break Gauge**. This meter fills as you deal and receive damage, providing access to powerful defensive and offensive tools.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Fatal Rush">
    A simple four-hit combo performed by pressing the Special button. The final hit causes a Fatal Stun, leaving the opponent completely vulnerable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Break Hold">
    By using 50% of your Break Gauge, you can perform a universal hold that works against high, mid, and low strikes. This is your primary escape tool when caught in a juggle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Break Blow">
    A devastating strike that requires 100% Break Gauge. It can parry incoming strikes and deal massive damage, often turning the tide of a round.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Hold Timing">
Attempting a hold at the wrong height (High, Mid, or Low) will leave you open to a "Counter" or "Hi Counter" throw, which can deplete a significant portion of your health bar.
</Callout>

</Section>

For PC players, the **Dead or Alive 6 Last Round** update introduces higher system requirements compared to the 2019 original. This is due to the enhanced textures, improved lighting, and the inclusion of the new Photo Mode features. Ensuring your hardware meets these standards is critical for a smooth 60 FPS experience, which is mandatory for competitive fighting games.

| **DirectX** | Version 12 | Version 12 |

Online play in Core Fighters is robust, supporting up to 16 players in a single lobby. However, it is important to note that **crossplay is not supported** between different platforms (e.g., PS5 cannot play against Steam users). Additionally, there is no cross-generational play between the original DOA6 and the Last Round update.

<Callout type="info" title="Network Stability">
Always use a wired Ethernet connection for online play. Fighting games require frame-perfect synchronization, and Wi-Fi jitter can cause significant "rollback" or lag spikes that ruin the experience for both players.
</Callout>
