<Section icon={Swords} title="The Core Combat: Understanding the Triangle System">

The most vital part of any **dead or alive 6 beginner guide** is the **Triangle System**. Every interaction in the game is governed by this three-way relationship. Understanding this will stop you from "button mashing" and start you on the path to making tactical "reads" on your opponent.

### The Rock-Paper-Scissors of DOA6
1.  **Strikes beat Throws**: If an opponent tries to grab you while you are mid-punch or kick, your strike will land and deal "Hi Counter" damage.
2.  **Throws beat Holds**: If an opponent is waiting to "Hold" (counter) your strike, they are vulnerable to a throw, which deals massive damage against defensive stances.
3.  **Holds beat Strikes**: If you predict the height of an incoming strike (High, Mid, or Low), you can perform a Hold to catch the limb and transition into a counter-attack.

| Interaction | Winner | Bonus Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strike vs Throw** | Strike | Interrupts grab, deals Hi Counter damage |
| **Throw vs Hold** | Throw | Maximum damage, punishes defensive mashing |
| **Hold vs Strike** | Hold | Reverses momentum, shifts frame advantage |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
Do not rely solely on strikes. New players often get "Hold-spammed" by intermediate players. If you notice your opponent is catching all your punches, stop attacking and use a **Throw** instead.
</Callout>

</Section>

### Defense and Guarding
Holding the **Hold (H)** button allows you to guard. You must choose between standing guard (blocks High and Mid strikes) and crouching guard (blocks Low strikes and avoids High strikes).

| Guard Type | Input | Protects Against | Vulnerable To |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standing Guard** | Back + H | High/Mid Strikes | Low Strikes, All Throws |
| **Crouching Guard** | Down-Back + H | Low Strikes | Mid Strikes, Low Throws |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Mechanics: Break Gauge and Fatal Stun">

As you move past the basics, you must learn to manage the **Break Gauge**. This blue bar fills up as you deal and receive damage. It is your most powerful resource for both offense and defense.

### How to use the Break Gauge
- **Break Blow (100% Meter)**: Forward + Special. A powerful strike that parries incoming attacks and deals massive damage. If it hits, it triggers a cinematic finish.
- **Break Hold (50% Meter)**: Back + Special. This is your "Get Out of Jail Free" card. It will counter **any** strike height (High, Mid, or Low), allowing you to escape a deadly combo.
