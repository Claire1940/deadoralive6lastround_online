<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering the Combat Triangle on PC">

The combat in Dead or Alive 6 is defined by the "Triangle System." This is a sophisticated rock-paper-scissors mechanic that dictates how every interaction is resolved. On a keyboard, your reaction time for hitting the "Hold" or "Throw" keys determines whether you take massive counter-damage or turn the tide of the match.

When using **dead or alive 6 keyboard controls**, you must be aware of the height of incoming attacks. Unlike 2D games where you simply block, DOA6 requires you to input specific "Holds" (J key + Direction) to catch strikes.

| Interaction | Winner | Bonus Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strike vs Throw** | **Strike** | Deals Hi-Counter damage / Stops throw attempt |
| **Throw vs Hold** | **Throw** | Deals massive Hi-Counter damage / Unescapable |
| **Hold vs Strike** | **Hold** | Reverses attack / Resets neutral or grants stun |

<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced Mechanics: Break Gauge and Special Moves">

The Break Gauge is a special meter introduced in the sixth installment that allows for powerful cinematic attacks and emergency defensive maneuvers. Managing this meter using your keyboard's **Special (I)** button is what separates beginners from intermediate players.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Fatal Rush (I, I, I, I)">
    A simple four-hit combo performed by tapping the Special key. On the fourth hit, if your gauge is full, it automatically converts into a Break Blow.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Break Blow (Forward + I)">
    Requires 100% Break Gauge. This is a powerful strike that can parry incoming attacks. On keyboard, ensure you are holding the "Forward" arrow key while pressing "I".
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Break Hold (Back + I)">
    Requires 50% Break Gauge. This is a universal hold that works against High, Mid, and Low strikes. It is your "get out of jail free" card when trapped in a Fatal Stun.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Side Attack (Up/Down + I)">
    Use this while sidestepping to dodge linear attacks and counter with a fast strike. It is highly effective against "bruiser" style characters like Diego or Bass.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Gauge Management">
Do not mash the Fatal Rush (I) key blindly. If the opponent blocks the first two hits, the third hit is often punishable. In the 2026 Last Round update, the recovery time on blocked Fatal Rushes has been slightly increased to discourage mashing.
</Callout>
