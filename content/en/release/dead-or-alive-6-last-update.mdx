<Section icon={Target} title="Combat System: The Triangle and Break Gauge">

The core of the **dead or alive 6 last update** remains the sophisticated "Triangle System." This rock-paper-scissors mechanic dictates every interaction: Strikes beat Throws, Throws beat Holds, and Holds beat Strikes. Mastering this, alongside the Break Gauge introduced in the sixth installment, is the key to victory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Learn the Triangle">
    Memorize the hierarchy. If your opponent is constantly holding (countering), use a Throw for massive "Hi-Counter" damage. If they are aggressive with strikes, time your Hold.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manage the Break Gauge">
    The meter fills as you take or deal damage. At 50%, you can perform a **Break Hold**, which counters any strike height (High, Mid, Low). At 100%, you can unleash a **Break Blow**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize Fatal Rush">
    The Special button (R1/RB) allows for a simple 4-hit combo. The final hit causes a "Fatal Stun," which prevents the opponent from using normal holds, forcing them to use meter to escape.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Navigate Danger Zones">
    Every stage has hazards. Use knockback strikes to push enemies into explosive floors, walls, or even crowds to extend your combos and maximize damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The 2026 update has refined the input buffer, making the execution of "Side Attacks" (Sidestep + Special) feel more responsive. This is crucial for avoiding linear, high-damage strings from characters like Jann Lee or Rig.

<Callout type="info" title="Break Hold Strategy">
Do not waste your Break Gauge on Break Blows if you are struggling defensively. A well-timed Break Hold can stop a game-ending combo, giving you the chance to reset the neutral game.
</Callout>

</Section>
