<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Break Gauge Resource">

The **Break Gauge** is a blue meter located beneath your health bar. It is the fuel for your most potent **dead or alive 6 special moves**. Understanding how to manage this resource is vital, as it governs both your ultimate offensive strike and your most reliable defensive escape. The gauge fills as you land hits, take damage, or successfully parry attacks.

| Move Name | Input | Gauge Cost | Property |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Break Blow** | **6 + S (Forward + S)** | **100%** | High-damage cinematic strike; parries incoming strikes. |
| **Break Hold** | **4 + S (Back + S)** | **50%** | Universal hold; counters High, Mid, and Low strikes. |
| **Fatal Rush** | **S, S, S, S** | **0% to 100%** | Four-hit combo; final hit becomes Break Blow if gauge is full. |
| **Side Attack** | **2+S or 8+S** | **0%** | Evasive strike that tracks opponents during sidesteps. |

The **Break Blow** is your "Super" move. When executed with a full meter, your character will perform a powerful strike that can absorb an opponent's incoming attack and trigger a cinematic sequence for massive damage. Interestingly, you can "Cancel" a Break Blow after the initial hit by pressing the Hold button, leaving the opponent in a "Fatal Stun" state and allowing for a customized follow-up combo.

<Callout type="warning" title="Gauge Management">
Do not spend your entire Break Gauge on a Break Blow if you are low on health. Saving 50% for a **Break Hold** is often the better tactical choice, as it allows you to escape an opponent's combo even while you are stunned.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Fatal Rush and Combo Integration">

The **Fatal Rush** is perhaps the most beginner-friendly addition to the Dead or Alive 6 special moves library. By simply tapping the Special button repeatedly, any character can perform a stylish, multi-hit combo. However, for advanced players, the Fatal Rush is a tool for "Fatal Stun," a state where the opponent cannot use standard holds to escape.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate the Rush">
    Press the **Special (S)** button to land the first hit. This move usually has decent reach and can punish whiffed attacks from the opponent.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Apply Fatal Stun">
    Continue pressing **S**. The second and third hits will lock the opponent into a **Fatal Stun**. During this window, the opponent is helpless unless they spend 50% of their own Break Gauge to perform a Break Hold.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Decide the Ender">
    On the fourth press of **S**, the character will perform a knockback strike. If your Break Gauge is at 100%, this final hit automatically converts into a full **Break Blow** cinematic.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Advanced Cancel">
    Instead of finishing the Fatal Rush, try stopping after the third hit and transitioning into a **Launcher** (like 3K or 8P) to start a more damaging air juggle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Character Variance">
While the inputs are the same, the speed of a Fatal Rush varies. Characters like **Kasumi** and **NiCO** have incredibly fast rushes, whereas power characters like **Diego** have slower hits with higher individual damage and better wall-splat potential.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Defensive Special Moves: The Break Hold">

In the Dead or Alive series, the "Hold" system is a unique defensive mechanic where you counter an opponent's strike by matching its height (High, Mid, or Low). However, guessing incorrectly leads to "Counter Hit" damage. The **Break Hold** (Back + S) removes the guesswork by providing a universal counter that works against all strike heights.

  <Card title="Standard Holds">

    - High risk if you misread the opponent

  </Card>
  <Card title="Break Hold (Back + S)">
    - **Universal coverage** (High/Mid/Low)

    - Lower damage, but resets the neutral game
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The Break Hold is your "get out of jail free" card. In the 2026 Last Round meta, top-tier players often hoard their meter specifically for this move to prevent opponents from landing high-damage "Wall Combos" or "Danger Zone" explosions.

| Defensive Scenario | Recommended Move | Reasoning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Predictable High Kick** | Standard High Hold (7H) | No cost; maximum punish damage. |
| **Trapped in Fatal Stun** | **Break Hold (4+S)** | Only way to escape Fatal Stun pressure. |
| **Opponent Sidestepping** | Side Attack (2/8+S) | Tracks the opponent and stops their movement. |
| **Pressure near Wall** | Break Blow (6+S) | Armor properties can blast through the pressure. |

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Tip: The Break Blow Cancel">
If you land the first hit of a Break Blow but realize it won't kill the opponent, press the **Hold (H)** button immediately. You will lose the cinematic damage but keep the opponent in a state where you can finish them with a faster, more efficient combo.
</Callout>
