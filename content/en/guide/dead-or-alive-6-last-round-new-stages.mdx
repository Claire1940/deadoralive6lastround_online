<Section icon={Swords} title="Interactive Combat Mechanics and Hazards">

The combat in Dead or Alive 6 Last Round new stages is defined by two core interactive systems: **Rumble Danger** and **Mass-Destruction**. These mechanics ensure that the environment is just as dangerous as the opponent. Understanding how to trigger these events is essential for high-level play, as they can provide the necessary damage boost to close out a round.

**Rumble Danger** occurs when a fighter is blown into the surrounding crowd. Rather than simply hitting a wall, the spectators will actively push the fighter back into the ring. This results in a unique stagger animation that creates a massive opening for the attacker. 

**Mass-Destruction** refers to the destructible objects scattered across the arenas. Items such as crates, jars, and fences can be broken. Unlike some other fighting games, these items do not regenerate during the round. Breaking them early can remove potential hazards that your opponent might try to use against you later.

| Mechanic | Trigger Condition | Gameplay Effect | Regeneration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rumble Danger** | Knockback into crowd | Staggers victim, creates opening | N/A |
| **Mass-Destruction** | Impact with stage objects | Extra damage, clears path | No |
| **Danger Zones** | Contact with floor/walls | Explosions, fire, or electricity | Periodic |

<Callout type="warning" title="Hazard Awareness">
Be extremely cautious on the **Chinese Festival** stage. The ground is littered with unused fireworks. A single heavy knockdown can trigger an explosion that deals significant chip damage to both players if they are within the blast radius.
</Callout>
